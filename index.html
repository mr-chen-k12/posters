Math Posters — Scan & Explore

Real-world math for 6th graders • Printed posters with QR codes that launch interactive explorations

Why this project? I wanted students to see that math is not just worksheets — it’s a tool we use to make sense of the world. Each classroom poster highlights a concept (ratios, scaling, geometry, data, etc.) and includes a QR code. When students scan the code, they jump into a short interactive that lets them test ideas, change values, and see the math do something.

Posters are designed to motivate curiosity first, then connect to grade-level standards through hands-on exploration.

Printing & QR Tips

  • Use any free QR generator with the interactive link (e.g., https://mr-chen-k12.github.io/posters/fractal.html).
  • Place the QR code below the poster title with a short prompt (e.g., “Scan to explore!”).
  • Recommended sizes: 18×24″ or 24×36″ posters; QR code at least 1.2–1.6″ square for easy scanning across the room.
  • Accessibility: include a short URL under the QR for students without a camera.
